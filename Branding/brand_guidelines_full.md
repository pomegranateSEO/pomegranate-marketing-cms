# pomegranate comprehensive brand guidelines

Status: working draft (main source file)

This project builds a full brand guidelines system. It is not a landing page build.

## 0) language standard

- Use plain English that is easy for people who use English as an additional language.
- Use British English spelling.
- Keep sentences short and direct.
- Define specialist terms on first use.
- Write so a young teenager who can use search and social platforms can still understand the message.
- Do not assume a founder already knows digital marketing, web design, search engine optimisation (SEO), or social media marketing jargon.
- If a term is technical, add a short plain-language explanation in the same sentence.

## 1) brand constitution

### 1.1 official name
- Always write the brand as `pomegranate` (lowercase only).

### 1.2 identity
- We are a strategy firm with clear principles.
- We choose truthful process over hype.
- Digital marketing is our umbrella niche.
- Our specific service pillars are search engine optimisation (SEO), web design, SEO training, and web design training.

### 1.3 mission
- To assist small businesses in going from darkness to light as they plant their digital seeds and grow through search engine optimisation (SEO, helping a business show up in search results), web design, SEO training (teaching teams how to improve search visibility), and web design training (teaching teams how to plan and improve website experience), by the permission of God Almighty.

### 1.4 moral pillars
- Truth over convenience.
- Integrity over manipulation.
- Service over ego.
- Useful outcomes over branding that only looks good.
- The best thing for us is the best thing for our clients. 
- Belief in a tomorrow which does not look like today for our clients.
- Neither us, nor our clients, nor anyone else knows what will happen in the immediate future. 
- We are not perfect, we should not take too much credit for wins as we also then will have to take credit for all the losses - when the truth is that we are not fully in control of either. 
- People do not make money. They work and they are rewarded with wealth.

## 4) messaging system

### 4.1 message hierarchy (top to bottom)
1. Mission anchor: To assist small businesses in going from darkness to light as they plant their digital seeds and grow.
2. Core value: We use truthful process, clear priorities, and measurable next steps.
3. Evidence point: Show one concrete proof point (for example, a result, process output, or approved case insight).
4. Offer statement: Explain what the client gets first and what happens next.
5. Primary CTA: Choose one dominant next action that matches the asset goal.
6. Secondary CTA offers: Keep as support only (for example, FAQ, resources, or alternate approved offers).

### 4.2 voice profile
- Core voice: clear, calm, practical, principled.
- We sound confident but never superior.
- We explain decisions, not slogans.
- We explain specialist terms in plain words before using shorthand or acronyms.
- We avoid drama, hype, and pressure language.
- We are the first to admit wrongdoing when we can.
- We are not perfect, we should not take too much credit for wins as we also then will have to take credit for all the losses - when the truth is that we are not fully in control of either.


### 4.3 tone by context
- Website and brochure: warm, bold, direct, and concise; focus on clarity and fit.
- Sales call follow-up: helpful and specific; confirm scope, risks, and next step.
- Proposal: precise and evidence-led; state assumptions and limits clearly.
- Social post: simple and useful; one idea per post, no inflated promises.
- Difficult update (delay or constraint): calm, honest, and accountable; give the recovery plan.

### 4.4 approved word choices
- Preferred verbs: `clarify`, `improve`, `plan`, `measure`, `support`, `deliver`.
- Preferred nouns: `evidence`, `outcome`, `priority`, `scope`, `decision`, `risk`.
- Prefer `digital marketing` as umbrella wording, then immediately name the specific service.
- Prefer `small businesses` over `SMEs` on first mention (define SME only if needed: small and medium-sized enterprises).
- Prefer `show up in search results` before `SEO performance` until SEO is clearly defined.
- Prefer `SEO training` and `web design training` for education offers, with plain-language explanation.
- Prefer `next step` over `quick win`.
- Prefer `realistic timeline` over `rapid transformation`.

### 4.5 banned or avoid phrases
- Do not use absolute claims such as `guaranteed growth`, `best in the world`, or `proven to always work`.
- Do not use manipulative urgency such as `act now or lose everything`.
- Do not use ego-led statements such as `we are geniuses` or `we are unmatched`.
- Avoid vague hype terms such as `game-changing`, `revolutionary`, or `magic` unless evidence is provided and approved.

### 4.6 integrity and evidence in messaging
- Every strong claim must include support in the same document or in a linked evidence source.
- Strong claim means a statement about measurable performance, market position, or guaranteed outcome.
- If evidence is not available, rewrite as a modest, humble and truthful statement.
- Time-bound results must include timeframe and conditions.

### 4.7 CTA architecture and approved wording library
- Multiple CTA offers are allowed, but only one CTA can be visually dominant as the primary action at a time.
- Primary CTA text must be selected from the approved CTA library.
- Secondary CTA offers may appear in support blocks and must keep secondary styling.
- If an asset is informational only, CTA may be omitted.
- In no-CTA mode, remove all promotional CTA prompts from that view and keep navigation purely informational.
- CTA format: verb + concrete next step + simple expectation.
- Keep CTA wording plain, specific, and non-hyped.
- Approved CTA library:
  - `Book a free 30-minute consultation`
  - `Book a clarity call`
  - `Get your free website audit`
  - `Get your free Google Business Profile audit`
  - `Get your free local SEO audit`
  - `Request your free homepage conversion review`
  - `Get a free keyword opportunity snapshot`
  - `Get a free technical SEO health check`
  - `Get your free lead-flow review`
  - `Request a free 90-day growth action plan`
  - `See your priority next steps`
  - `Start with a free strategy review`

### 4.8 signature usage in messaging contexts
- The signature appears once, at the end of major materials only.
- Do not place the signature in buttons, subject lines, headers, or repeated footers.
- Approved signature text: `Serving small businesses as their digital seeds go from darkness to light, if God Almighty is willing.`
- In short-form assets (ads, social captions, short emails), omit the signature unless the asset is the final major statement.

## 5) accessibility and hospitality standard

### 5.1 baseline
- Target WCAG 2.1 AAA (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) across digital materials.
- Apply this standard to websites, presentations, PDF outputs, and plain-text exports.

### 5.2 colour and contrast
- Body text contrast must be >= 7:1.
- Validate all palette and text pairings before approval.

### 5.3 readability and structure
- No body font size below 16px.
- Keep hierarchy consistent and easy to scan.
- Ensure copy remains understandable for ESL readers.
- Write at a level a young teenager can follow.
- Do not assume prior study of digital marketing, web design, search engine optimisation, or social media marketing.
- Replace jargon with plain wording, or define it immediately.

### 5.4 interaction expectations
- Keyboard accessibility and visible focus states are required.
- Touch targets for interactive controls must be at least `44x44px`.
- Avoid motion that impairs readability or orientation.

## 6) visual identity and usage system

### 6.1 visual theme guardrails
- Theme direction: practical clarity with calm contrast and minimalist futuristic technology style.
- Darkness -> Light is mandatory for major assets: start in darkness at the top and transition to light by the final sections.
- The progression must be visible in layout, surface treatment, and reading flow.
- Clarity always comes first. Decorative effects must not reduce reading comfort.
- Avoid visual noise: no crowded backgrounds, heavy texture, or low-contrast overlays behind body text.

### 6.2 colour system and tokens
- Use this base light-state token set in all brand materials:
  - `--colour-bg-primary`: Parchment Soil `#FAF8F5`
  - `--colour-text-primary`: Obsidian Seed `#000000`
  - `--colour-accent-primary`: Deep Seed Red `#4F0010`
  - `--colour-accent-secondary`: Deep Seed Red Alt `#7A0016`
  - `--colour-surface-muted`: Soft Sage `#B2C2AD`
  - `--colour-border-strong`: Obsidian Seed `#000000`
- Use this mandatory dark-start transition extension for major assets:
  - `--colour-dark-start`: Night Ink `#070B13`
  - `--colour-dark-mid`: Deep Slate `#111A2B`
  - `--colour-dark-rise`: Steel Horizon `#1E2C45`
  - `--colour-text-on-dark`: Light Cloud `#F7F9FF`
- Body text must only use colour pairs that pass `>= 7:1` contrast.
- Accent colours are for emphasis, not for long body paragraphs.
- Never communicate meaning by colour alone; pair with text labels or icons.

### 6.3 typography system
- Use a two-role typography system only (heading and body) to keep outputs consistent and readable.
- Primary pair (default): Fraunces for headings and Public Sans for body text.
- Fallback pair: Lora for headings and Inter for body text.
- Approved alternate pair (editorial trust contexts): Newsreader for headings and Roboto for body text.
- Approved alternate pair (accessibility-first contexts): Lexend for headings and Source Sans 3 for body text.
- Approved alternate pair (futuristic bold contexts): Bebas Neue for headings and Source Sans 3 for body text.
- Use one pair per asset; do not mix pairs in the same page, deck, or document.
- Minimum body size: 16px. Standard body size: 18px where layout allows.
- Line height: 1.5 to 1.7 for body text.
- Use weight 400 for body and 600 to 700 for headings.
- Keep paragraph width between 45 and 80 characters for readability.

### 6.4 spacing and layout rules
- Use an 8px spacing rhythm (`8, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48`).
- Design mobile-first: start from `320px` and `375px` widths, then scale up to larger breakpoints.
- Keep one clear primary action area per screen or page.
- Use consistent section padding so users can scan content quickly.
- Keep left alignment for long text blocks.
- Avoid full-width dense text walls; split with headings and short lists.

### 6.5 imagery and icon guidance
- Do not generate or use representational images of living beings, natural scenes, celestial objects, or physical objects.
- Use abstract geometric patterns, grids, gradients, line systems, and typographic composition for visual interest.
- Keep visual language minimalist, beautiful, and technology-forward.
- Icons must be simple, abstract, clear, and stylistically consistent across a document.
- Every meaningful non-text visual must include descriptive alternative text for assistive technologies.

### 6.6 component usage rules
- Buttons: one primary style and one secondary style only.
- Cards: keep title, short description, and optional supporting link in a consistent order.
- Forms: show labels above fields, clear error text, and required-field markers.
- Tables and charts: include clear headings, units, and source notes where relevant.
- Focus indicators must remain visible on interactive elements.

### 6.7 motion and reduced-complexity guidance
- Use motion only to support orientation (for example, reveal order or state change).
- Keep transitions short and calm (typically 150ms to 250ms).
- Avoid looping decorative motion that competes with reading.
- Provide a reduced-motion experience that removes non-essential animation.
- Provide a reduced-complexity content version for dense pages (shorter blocks, fewer concurrent choices).

### 6.8 do and do not rules
- Do keep layouts clean, readable, and calm.
- Do use tokens, spacing rhythm, and typography rules consistently.
- Do verify contrast before sign-off.
- Do not place text over complex image areas without a solid contrast-safe surface.
- Do not use visual styles that suggest exaggerated promises or unrealistic outcomes.

### 6.9 accessibility-safe visual checklist
- WCAG 2.1 AAA intent is required for all brand outputs.
- Body text contrast check recorded as `>= 7:1` before approval.
- Heading structure must remain logical (`H1` to `H3`) for assistive technology navigation.
- Text should stay clear at 200% zoom without loss of content or function.
- Mobile-first checks must pass at `320`, `375`, `768`, `1024`, and `1440` widths with no horizontal scroll.
- Interactive targets must meet `>= 44x44px` and keep visible focus indicators.
- Motion must have a reduced-complexity alternative.

## 7) management and workflow

### 7.1 operating model and ownership
- Governing file: `project_roadmap.md` defines mission, fixed principles, and mandatory audits.
- Tracking file: `roadmap_of_tasks.md` records task status, decision notes, and current focus.
- Master content file: `brand_guidelines_full.md` is the source for guideline content.
- Continuity file: `chat_context_handoff_2026-02-24.md` is the running handover log and must not be deleted.
- Role model: `@general` drafts and updates content; `@explore` runs independent audit passes and flags risks.

### 7.2 approval flow
1. Re-read `project_roadmap.md` (`R0.0`) and note principle alignment for the task.
2. Draft or update the target section in `brand_guidelines_full.md`.
3. Run required audits for that task and fix any failed items directly.
4. Update task status and concise decision notes in `roadmap_of_tasks.md`.
5. Add a revision line in `brand_guidelines_full.md`.
6. Add one dated handover line in `chat_context_handoff_2026-02-24.md` with changes, audits run, and next step.

### 7.3 audit cadence and triggers
- Trigger A1-A6 when a major section is added, restructured, or materially reworded.
- Trigger A3 whenever visual, layout, or interaction guidance changes.
- Trigger A4 whenever claims, outcomes, proof points, or proof wording changes.
- Trigger A5 whenever CTA text, placement, or document flow changes.
- Trigger A6 whenever signature wording or placement changes.
- Run a full A1-A6 sweep before final sign-off.

### 7.4 audit ownership and pass checks
| Audit | Owner | Trigger | Pass check |
|---|---|---|---|
| A1 Metaphor restraint | `@explore` | Messaging or narrative edits | Plain business wording first; no stacked farm/tree/leaf metaphor |
| A2 ESL clarity | `@explore` | Any text rewrite | Short direct sentences; no assumed jargon; terms defined on first use |
| A3 Accessibility growth | `@general` then `@explore` verify | Visual or interaction edits | WCAG 2.1 AAA intent documented, body text `>= 7:1`, reduced-complexity alternatives present |
| A4 Integrity and claims | `@general` then `@explore` verify | Claims or outcomes edits | No hype or unverified claims; evidence rule applied |
| A5 CTA architecture | `@general` then `@explore` verify | CTA edits | One dominant primary CTA per document or view with approved library text and clearly secondary support offers, or explicit informational no-CTA mode |

### 7.5 evidence requirements and records
- Strong claims need evidence in the same document or in a clearly linked source.
- Evidence should state source, timeframe, and conditions.
- If evidence is missing or weak, rewrite claim to a modest and truthful statement.
- Keep a short decision note in `roadmap_of_tasks.md` for every material wording decision.

### 7.6 exception handling
- Exceptions are allowed only when needed for legal, regulatory, or critical accessibility reasons.
- Every exception must include: reason, scope, owner, temporary or permanent status, and review date.
- Exceptions must not break fixed principles without explicit written approval in decision notes.
- Temporary exceptions require a planned return-to-standard action.

### 7.7 change control
- Make changes in small, reviewable updates tied to task IDs.
- Keep heading structure stable so presentation, `.txt`, and PDF export paths stay reliable.
- Do not remove existing history lines from revision logs or handover logs.
- If a change affects CTA or signature governance, run A5 and A6 in the same update cycle.

### 7.8 release checks and governance controls
- Pre-release check 1: fixed principles still hold, including lowercase `pomegranate` usage.
- Pre-release check 2: accessibility controls remain explicit (WCAG 2.1 AAA intent and body text `>= 7:1`).
- Pre-release check 3: one dominant primary CTA with clear secondary offers is present, or informational no-CTA mode is explicitly applied.
- Pre-release check 4: signature appears once at the end of major materials only.
- Pre-release check 5: decision notes, revision log, and handover line are fully updated.

### 7.9 handover and update responsibilities
- After each completed task, add one line under `## Handover update log` in `chat_context_handoff_2026-02-24.md`.
- The handover line must include: date, what changed, audits run, and next step.
- Keep updates concise so the next agent can continue without re-reading full history.
- Never delete prior handover entries.
